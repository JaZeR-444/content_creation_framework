# Glossary

## General Terms

*   **Content Pillars:** Core topics or themes around which a channel or content strategy is built, aligning with audience interests and brand goals.
*   **Ideal Viewer Profile:** A detailed description of the target audience, including demographics, motivations, pain points, and questions.
*   **Positioning Statement:** A concise statement defining a brand's unique value proposition for its target audience.
*   **Publishing Cadence:** A consistent and sustainable schedule for releasing new content.
*   **Brand One-Pager:** A single-page document summarizing a channel's brand identity, including voice, tone, colors, and visual style.
*   **Competitor Scan:** An analysis of competitor channels to identify content gaps, strategic opportunities, and unique positioning.
*   **Seed Keyword List:** A foundational list of terms and questions used for content planning and SEO.

## Production & Post-Production Terms

*   **B-roll:** Supplementary footage used to intercut with the main shot, often to add visual interest or cover edits.
*   **Checksum:** A value used to verify the integrity of data, ensuring that files have not been corrupted during transfer or storage.
*   **CTA (Call to Action):** An instruction to the audience designed to provoke an immediate response, e.g., "Subscribe," "Download Now."
*   **Hook:** The opening segment of a video or piece of content designed to immediately grab the viewer's attention.
*   **Logline:** A brief (one to two sentences) summary of a video's central conflict and premise.
*   **LUT (Look-Up Table):** A file containing mathematical instructions to change an image's color and tone, used for color grading.
*   **LUFS (Loudness Units Full Scale):** A standard for measuring audio loudness, ensuring consistent volume across different content.
*   **Metadata:** Data that provides information about other data, such as video titles, descriptions, tags, and chapters.
*   **NLE (Non-Linear Editor):** Software used for video editing (e.g., Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve) that allows non-destructive editing.
*   **Pre-Production Checklist:** A systematic list of tasks to complete before filming, covering planning, logistics, and creative elements.
*   **Proxy:** Lower-resolution versions of original media files used during editing to improve performance, later replaced by the high-res originals for export.
*   **Shot List:** A detailed list of every shot needed for a video, including camera angles, framing, and movement.
*   **SRT (SubRip Subtitle):** A common file format for subtitles, containing text and timestamps.
*   **Timecode:** A sequence of numerical code generated by a video or audio source, used for synchronization and logging.

## Optimization & Distribution Terms

*   **A/B Testing:** A method of comparing two versions of an asset (e.g., title, thumbnail) to see which performs better.
*   **Chapters:** Timestamps and labels within a video that allow viewers to navigate to specific sections.
*   **Carousel:** A multi-image post format, often used on social media, where users swipe through a series of images or graphics.
*   **Distribution Plan:** A strategy outlining how and where content will be shared across various platforms.
*   **End Screens:** Customizable elements displayed at the end of a YouTube video to promote other content, playlists, or calls to action.
*   **Hashtags:** Keywords or phrases preceded by a hash symbol (#), used on social media to categorize content and aid discoverability.
*   **Info Cards:** Interactive elements that appear during a YouTube video, linking to other videos, playlists, or external sites.
*   **Keyword Selection:** The process of choosing relevant keywords to optimize content for search engines and audience discoverability.
*   **Playlist Assignment:** Organizing videos into curated playlists to improve session watch time and viewer experience.
*   **Quote Image:** A graphic featuring a compelling quote, often from the content, designed for social media sharing.
*   **Repurpose Workflow:** A process for transforming long-form content into multiple shorter, platform-specific assets.
*   **Thumbnail Checklist:** A set of guidelines to ensure thumbnails are visually appealing, informative, and optimized for click-through.
*   **Title Sprint:** A rapid brainstorming and selection process for generating high-performing video titles.
*   **UTM Parameters:** Tags added to URLs to track the effectiveness of marketing campaigns across traffic sources and mediums.

## Analysis & Iteration Terms

*   **APV (Average Percent Viewed):** The average percentage of a video that viewers watch.
*   **AVD (Average View Duration):** The average amount of time viewers spend watching a video.
*   **Cohort:** A group of videos or users sharing a common characteristic (e.g., published in the same month, similar length) used for comparative analysis.
*   **CTR (Click-Through Rate):** The percentage of impressions that result in a click or view.
*   **Experiment Cadence:** A regular schedule for implementing and measuring intentional changes to content elements.
*   **Impressions:** The number of times a content's thumbnail or preview was displayed to users.
*   **KPI (Key Performance Indicator):** A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively a company is achieving key business objectives.
*   **PII (Personally Identifiable Information):** Information that can be used to identify an individual (e.g., email, phone number).
*   **Refresh Winners:** The process of updating high-performing content with new metadata or visuals to extend its lifespan and boost performance.
*   **SLA (Service Level Agreement):** A commitment between a service provider and a client, defining the level of service expected.
*   **Sentiment Analysis:** The process of determining the emotional tone behind a piece of text, often used for comments and feedback.
*   **Traffic Sources:** The different channels or platforms from which viewers discover content (e.g., YouTube Search, Browse Features, External).
*   **Underperformer:** Content that does not meet predefined performance benchmarks after a certain period.
