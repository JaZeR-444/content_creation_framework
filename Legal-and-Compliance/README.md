## Key Legal Considerations

### Copyright
- Only use content you own or have licensed
- Understand fair use (limited protection)
- Get written permission when needed
- Track all licenses and expirations

### Music Licensing
- YouTube Audio Library (free for YouTube)
- Royalty-free purchased tracks
- Subscription services (Epidemic Sound, Artlist)
- Public domain music
- Creative Commons (check restrictions)

### Stock Assets
- Verify commercial use rights
- Save license documentation
- Provide attribution if required
- Track license expiration dates

### Trademarks
- Don't use others' brand names without permission
- Avoid implying endorsements
- Use disclaimers when reviewing products
- Respect brand guidelines

### Privacy and Likeness
- Get releases for identifiable people
- Don't use private information
- Blur faces if consent not obtained
- Respect location privacy

### Disclosures (FTC Requirements)

Required disclosures for:
- **Sponsored content** - Clearly label paid partnerships
- **Affiliate links** - Disclose commission relationships
- **Free products** - Mention if product was gifted
- **Financial interests** - Disclose if you own stock, work for company, etc.

**Disclosure requirements:**
- Clear and conspicuous
- Near relevant content (not just in description)
- Plain language ("Ad," "Sponsored," "Paid partnership")
- Can't be hidden or easily missed

## Common Compliance Issues

### Music Copyright Strikes
- **Problem:** Using copyrighted music without license
- **Solution:** Use licensed music, YouTube Audio Library, or royalty-free tracks
- **Documentation:** Track all music licenses in Attribution-and-Licenses.md

### Stock Asset Misuse
- **Problem:** Using assets outside license terms
- **Solution:** Verify license allows your use case
- **Documentation:** Save all license files, track in Attribution-and-Licenses.md

### Inadequate Disclosures
- **Problem:** Not clearly disclosing sponsored content
- **Solution:** Use platform tools (YouTube paid promotion, IG partnership label)
- **Documentation:** Note all sponsorships and partnerships

### Unsubstantiated Claims
- **Problem:** Making claims without evidence
- **Solution:** Use Claims-Checklist.md before publishing
- **Documentation:** Keep sources and research

### Fair Use Misunderstanding
- **Problem:** Assuming fair use protects all commentary
- **Solution:** Understand fair use is limited; get legal advice for risky content
- **Documentation:** Keep notes on fair use justification

## Documentation Best Practices

### 1. Be Thorough
- Document everything, even if it seems minor
- Keep original files and licenses
- Screenshot license pages
- Save email confirmations

### 2. Be Organized
- Use Attribution-and-Licenses.md consistently
- Link to Content-IDs
- Update in real-time, not retroactively

### 3. Be Accessible
- Store in cloud (Google Drive, Dropbox)
- Keep backups
- Make available to team members
- Easy to search and reference

### 4. Be Proactive
- Review before publishing
- Don't wait for problems
- Update as laws/policies change
- Consult professionals when uncertain

## When to Consult a Lawyer

Seek legal advice for:
- Complex fair use situations
- Product reviews with strong criticism
- Content about legal matters
- Potential defamation issues
- Contract negotiations (sponsorships, partnerships)
- Copyright disputes
- Business formation and structure
- International distribution concerns

**Disclaimer:** This framework provides organizational tools, not legal advice. Consult qualified legal professionals for specific situations.
